Ecommerce, or electronic commerce, is the practice of buying and selling goods and services over the internet. It encompasses all online activities related to the exchange of products and services, whether between businesses (B2B), businesses and consumers (B2C), or consumers themselves (C2C). Essentially, it's the digital version of traditional commerce. 
Here's a more detailed look:
Key Aspects of Ecommerce:
Online Transactions:
Ecommerce relies on online platforms like websites, mobile apps, or online marketplaces to facilitate transactions. 
Diverse Products and Services:
Ecommerce covers the sale of physical goods, digital products, and various services like online tutoring or delivery services. 
Business Models:
Ecommerce can involve different business models, such as:
B2B (Business-to-Business): Businesses selling to other businesses. 
B2C (Business-to-Consumer): Businesses selling directly to consumers. 
C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer): Consumers selling to other consumers, often facilitated by platforms like eBay or Craigslist. 
Growth and Impact:
Ecommerce is a rapidly growing sector, with projected sales exceeding $6 trillion in 2026. It has significantly impacted how businesses operate and how consumers shop, offering convenience, accessibility, and a wider range of choices. 
Examples:
Online retailers like Amazon and Etsy, food delivery platforms, and businesses offering services online are all examples of ecommerce
